**SQL Sales Data Analysis – Amazon E-Commerce Case Study
Project Overview**
You are working as a Data Analyst at Amazon, a leading global e-commerce platform. The management team has requested a detailed analysis of product sales data to better understand pricing strategies, discounts, product performance, and customer feedback.
This project uses SQL (MySQL) to analyze structured sales and review data by writing queries that answer real-world business questions. The goal is to extract actionable insights that can support pricing, marketing, and product strategy decisions.

**Dataset**
The dataset contains information related to:
Product names and categories
Actual prices and discounted prices
Discount percentages
Product ratings
Customer review content

**Business Questions Solved**
The following SQL queries were written to address management’s requirements:
List all products with a discounted price below ₹500
Find products with a discount percentage of 50% or more
Retrieve all products where the product name contains the word "Cable"
Display the difference between the average actual price and the average discounted price for each product
Query customer reviews that mention "fast charging"
Identify products with a discount percentage between 20% and 40%
Find products with an actual price above ₹1,000 and a rating of 4 stars or higher
Find products where the discounted price ends with the digit 9
Display review content containing negative keywords such as worst, waste, poor, or not good
List all products where the category includes "Accessories"

**Key Insights**
Identified products using aggressive discounting strategies
Highlighted premium and high-rated products
Extracted positive and negative customer sentiment from reviews
Analyzed pricing gaps between actual and discounted prices
Supported decision-making related to promotions and product positioning

Support business decisions through data-driven analysis
